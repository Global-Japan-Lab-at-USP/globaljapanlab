Global Japan Lab

This initiative aims to introduce Global Japan Studies at the University of São Paulo (USP) by fostering interdisciplinary methodologies, multilingual pedagogy, and sustained engagement in international research networks. It represents a fundamental rethinking of Japan Studies that transcends conventional area studies by de-centering nation-bound narratives to instead locate the study of Japan within truly comparative and transnational frameworks.

グローバル日本研究ラボ

この取り組みは、学際的な方法論、多言語教育学、国際研究ネットワークへの持続的な参画を促進することにより、サンパウロ大学（USP）にグローバル日本研究を導入することを目的としています。これは、国家に縛られた物語を脱中心化し、真に比較的で超国家的な枠組みの中に日本研究を位置づけることで、従来の地域研究を超越する日本研究の根本的な再考を表しています。

Laboratório Global do Japão

Esta iniciativa visa introduzir os Estudos Globais do Japão na Universidade de São Paulo (USP), promovendo metodologias interdisciplinares, pedagogia multilíngue e engajamento sustentado em redes internacionais de pesquisa. Representa um repensar fundamental dos Estudos Japoneses que transcende os estudos regionais convencionais, descentralizando narrativas limitadas por fronteiras nacionais para localizar o estudo do Japão dentro de estruturas verdadeiramente comparativas e transnacionais.

About This Project

This website serves as a static prototype for the classified Fapesp International Thematic Grant application by Dr. Seth Jacobowitz (PI) and Drs. Leiko Matsubara Morales, Silvio Miyazaki, Alexandre Uehara, and Lillian Yamamoto (Co-PIs). Integral to establishing a cutting-edge Global Japan Studies program at USP, this project proposes targeted investments in academic resources, student and faculty research support, and digital infrastructure.

The functional database-driven version of this platform will be developed upon grant funding, incorporating the full range of research tools and collaborative features outlined in our FAPESP International Thematic Grant proposal.

プロジェクトについて

このウェブサイトは、セス・ヤコボウィッツ博士（主任研究者）および共同研究者であるレイコ・マツバラ・モラレス博士、シルビオ・ミヤザキ博士、アレクサンドレ・ウエハラ博士、リリアン・ヤマモト博士によるFAPESP国際テーマ別助成金申請の静的プロトタイプとして機能します。USPにおける最先端のグローバル日本研究プログラムの設立に不可欠な本プロジェクトは、学術資源、学生・教員研究支援、デジタルインフラへの戦略的投資を提案しています。

このプラットフォームの機能的なデータベース駆動版は、助成金の資金提供により開発され、FAPESP国際テーマ別助成金提案書に概説された研究ツールと協力機能の全範囲を組み込む予定です。

Sobre Este Projeto

Este website serve como um protótipo estático para a candidatura classificada ao Auxílio Temático Internacional da FAPESP pelo Dr. Seth Jacobowitz (Pesquisador Principal) e pelos Drs. Leiko Matsubara Morales, Silvio Miyazaki, Alexandre Uehara e Lillian Yamamoto (Co-Pesquisadores Principais). Integral ao estabelecimento de um programa de ponta em Estudos Globais do Japão na USP, este projeto propõe investimentos direcionados em recursos acadêmicos, apoio à pesquisa estudantil e docente, e infraestrutura digital.

A versão funcional desta plataforma baseada em banco de dados será desenvolvida mediante o financiamento da bolsa, incorporando toda a gama de ferramentas de pesquisa e recursos colaborativos delineados em nossa proposta de Auxílio Temático Internacional da FAPESP.

Research Methodologies

研究方法論

Metodologias de Pesquisa

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Facilitates research across economics, linguistics, literature, and international relations through common analytical frameworks while maintaining methodological consistency.

学際的協力

経済学、言語学、文学、国際関係学における研究を、方法論的一貫性を維持しながら共通の分析枠組みを通じて促進します。

Colaboração Interdisciplinar

Facilita pesquisas em economia, linguística, literatura e relações internacionais através de estruturas analíticas comuns, mantendo consistência metodológica.

Social Sciences

Our researchers integrate empirical and statistical methods to investigate global geopolitics, trade policy, models of economic cooperation, and Japan's role in international systems.

社会科学

私たちの研究者は、実証的・統計的手法を統合し、グローバル地政学、貿易政策、経済協力モデル、国際システムにおける日本の役割を調査します。

Ciências Sociais

Nossos pesquisadores integram métodos empíricos e estatísticos para investigar geopolítica global, política comercial, modelos de cooperação econômica e o papel do Japão em sistemas internacionais.

Humanities

Our researchers apply theoretical frameworks such as structural linguistics, media history, diaspora studies, gender studies, and memory studies.

人文学

私たちの研究者は、構造言語学、メディア史、ディアスポラ研究、ジェンダー研究、記憶研究などの理論的枠組みを適用します。

Humanidades

Nossos pesquisadores aplicam estruturas teóricas como linguística estrutural, história da mídia, estudos da diáspora, estudos de gênero e estudos da memória.

Transnational Diaspora in Scalable Analysis

Enables systematic analysis of large corpora such as the decades' worth of Japanese Brazilian newspapers in Stanford University's Hoji Shinbun Digital Collection, identifying patterns that would require hundreds of hours of conventional research.

拡張可能な分析における超国家的ディアスポラ

スタンフォード大学の邦字新聞デジタルコレクションの数十年分の日系ブラジル新聞など、大規模コーパスの体系的分析を可能にし、従来の研究では数百時間を要するパターンを特定します。

Diáspora Transnacional em Análise Escalável

Permite análise sistemática de grandes corpora, como décadas de jornais nipo-brasileiros na Coleção Digital Hoji Shinbun da Universidade de Stanford, identificando padrões que exigiriam centenas de horas de pesquisa convencional.

Global Economic & Political Dynamics

Bridges Brazil-Japan relations to investigate trade policies within transnational networks and applying combining archival research and quantitative data analysis for macro-economic governance and international affairs.

グローバル経済・政治動態

ブラジル・日本関係を架橋し、超国家的ネットワーク内の貿易政策を調査し、マクロ経済ガバナンスと国際問題のためのアーカイブ研究と定量的データ分析を組み合わせて適用します。

Dinâmicas Econômicas e Políticas Globais

Conecta as relações Brasil-Japão para investigar políticas comerciais dentro de redes transnacionais, aplicando e combinando pesquisa arquivística e análise quantitativa de dados para governança macroeconômica e assuntos internacionais.

Applied Linguistics and Language Education

Investigates the revitalization of heritage Japanese through sociolinguistic analysis of bilingualism and culturally responsive pedagogy.

応用言語学・言語教育

バイリンガリズムの社会言語学的分析と文化的に応答性のある教育学を通じて、継承日本語の活性化を調査します。

Linguística Aplicada e Educação Linguística

Investiga a revitalização do japonês como língua de herança através da análise sociolinguística do bilinguismo e pedagogia culturalmente responsiva.

Research Innovation

The creation of Global Japan Lab website will serve as a central platform for hosting a keyword searchable, open-access, and multilingual bibliography and glossary for Japan Studies in Brazil. The Global Japan Lab will also feature a works-in-progress lecture series led by project team members and including the postdoctoral fellow. Contingent upon funding support by USP and FAPESP, we propose a longer-term program of conference panels, workshops, and invited lectures by prominent Japan Studies scholars from Brazil, Japan, and around the world.

研究革新

グローバル日本研究ラボのウェブサイトの創設は、ブラジルの日本研究のためのキーワード検索可能で、オープンアクセスの多言語書誌・用語集を提供する中央プラットフォームとして機能します。グローバル日本研究ラボはまた、チームメンバーやポスドクを含む進行中の研究発表シリーズを開催させていただきます。USPとFAPESPの資金支援を条件として、ブラジル、日本、世界各地の著名な日本研究学者による長期的な会議パネル、ワークショップ、招聘講演プログラムを提案します。

Inovação em Pesquisa

A criação do website do Laboratório Global do Japão servirá como uma plataforma central para hospedar uma bibliografia e glossário multilíngue, de acesso aberto e pesquisável por palavras-chave para os Estudos Japoneses no Brasil. O Laboratório Global do Japão também apresentará uma série de palestras de trabalhos em andamento liderada por membros da equipe do projeto e incluindo o pós-doutorando. Dependente do apoio de financiamento da USP e FAPESP, propomos um programa de longo prazo de painéis de conferência, workshops e palestras convidadas por estudiosos proeminentes de Estudos Japoneses do Brasil, Japão e do mundo todo.

Research Team

Our interdisciplinary team brings together leading scholars in Japanese studies, linguistics, economics, literature, and international relations to pioneer innovative global methodologies.

Principal Investigators

Seth Jacobowitz

Principal Investigator

Dr. Jacobowitz specializes in modern Japanese literature, visual culture, and media history. In addition to his work on transnational Japanese diaspora, he is also developing AI-assisted research methodologies for the Global Japan Lab. He previously taught at Yale, Texas State, and San Francisco State University.

Leiko Matsubara Morales

Co-Principal Investigator - Linguistics

Associate Professor specializing in Japanese-Brazilian sociolinguistics and heritage language education. Her research examines ethnolinguistic vitality among Japanese-Brazilian communities and the development of culturally responsive pedagogical approaches for multilingual contexts.

Silvio Miyazaki

Co-Principal Investigator - Economics

Professor of International Economics focusing on ASEAN-Latin America economic integration and Japanese business practices in Brazil. His research analyzes trade policies within transnational networks and macro-economic governance structures.

Alexandre Uehara

Co-Principal Investigator - International Relations

Associate Professor specializing in Indo-Pacific geopolitics, Japan-US security relations, and disability studies in Japan. His work examines regional security architectures and their impact on international cooperation frameworks.

Lilian Yamamoto

Co-Principal Investigator - Literature

Assistant Professor of Japanese Literature focusing on hibakusha literature, gender studies, and trauma narratives. Her research examines female representation in atomic bomb literature and comparative literary structures between Japanese and Western texts.

Helder Nakaya

Research Associate

Dr. Nakaya is a Senior Researcher at Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein in São Paulo and Associate Professor at the University of São Paulo, Brazil. He holds a PhD in Molecular Biology and has extensive training in Bioinformatics. He will participate in the Global Japan Lab as an advisor on implementing AI-assisted methodologies and research.

Henoch Mandelbaum

Research Associate

Doctoral candidate in Political Science at USP, specializing in international relations and Japanese foreign policy. Henoch provides essential research support and helps bridge the project's political science components with broader interdisciplinary objectives.

Research Initiatives

Our research initiatives represent innovative approaches to Global Japan Studies, integrating AI-assisted methodologies with traditional scholarly practices to create new frameworks for transnational research.

Current Initiatives

Digital Diaspora Archive Project

Leveraging AI tools to analyze decades of Japanese-Brazilian newspapers from Stanford's Hoji Shinbun Digital Collection. This initiative demonstrates scalable archival research methodologies that identify patterns requiring hundreds of hours using conventional approaches.

Multilingual Bibliography Platform

Development of a comprehensive, keyword-searchable database for Japan Studies research in Brazil. The platform will provide open-access resources in Portuguese, Japanese, and English, facilitating international scholarly collaboration.

Heritage Language Revitalization

Systematic study of Japanese language maintenance among Brazilian communities, developing culturally responsive pedagogical frameworks for multilingual education contexts and community-based language preservation programs.

Brazil-Japan Economic Integration Analysis

Comprehensive research on trade policies and economic cooperation models between Brazil and Japan within broader transnational networks, combining archival research with quantitative data analysis for policy recommendations.

Comparative Literature and Memory Studies

Cross-cultural analysis of trauma narratives and memory representation in Japanese and Brazilian literary traditions, with particular focus on hibakusha literature and gender studies approaches to historical memory.

AI-Assisted Research Methodology Training

Developing comprehensive training protocols for humanities scholars to effectively integrate AI tools into traditional research practices, creating sustainable models for technological adoption in academic contexts.

Future Directions

The Global Japan Lab envisions expanding these initiatives through sustained international partnerships, postdoctoral research programs, and collaborative networks with institutions in Japan, the United States, and other regions with significant Japanese diaspora populations.

Contingent upon FAPESP funding, we propose establishing regular conference panels, workshops, and visiting scholar programs that position USP as a leading center for innovative Japan Studies research in Latin America.

Digital Archives & Databases

Comprehensive digital archive of Japanese diaspora newspapers, providing decades of historical documentation for systematic analysis.

Repository of Brazilian academic research including significant collections on Japanese-Brazilian studies and international relations.

Essential demographic and economic data for analyzing Japanese-Brazilian communities and bilateral trade relationships.

AI Research Tools

Large Language Models

Advanced AI assistants for text analysis, translation support, and research methodology development. Essential for processing large corpora and identifying patterns in multilingual datasets.

Japanese OCR Technology

Specialized optical character recognition tools for digitizing historical Japanese texts and newspapers, enabling systematic analysis of previously inaccessible archival materials.

Statistical Analysis Platforms

Computational tools for quantitative analysis of economic data, demographic trends, and linguistic patterns in Japanese-Brazilian communities and bilateral trade relationships.

Digital Humanities Frameworks

Integrated platforms combining traditional humanities research methods with computational approaches for textual analysis, network mapping, and collaborative annotation systems.
